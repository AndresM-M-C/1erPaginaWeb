What are they?

The Seven Wonders of the World are a set of remarkable architectural, artistic, and historical achievements that have been recognized as iconic and exceptional monuments of antiquity. Throughout history, there have been several lists of wonders of the world, but the most famous one is the list of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The original list of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World was compiled in the 2nd century BC by the Greek poet Antipater of Sidon. These wonders were considered the most impressive and grandiose monuments of their time and have fascinated subsequent generations. However, unfortunately, most of these wonders no longer exist today due to erosion, destruction, or the passage of time.

Why are they considered "wonders" of the world?

The Seven Wonders of the World are considered "wonders" because of their exceptional beauty, grandeur, and historical significance. Each of these architectural structures and monuments represents an impressive achievement of engineering, design, and human skill of the era in which they were created. These wonders are enduring testimonies to the abilities and creativity of ancient civilizations. They are often characterized by their monumental size, intricate details, advanced construction techniques, and their impact on society and culture at that time. Furthermore, the Seven Wonders of the World have survived through the centuries and have captivated subsequent generations, making them iconic symbols and a source of admiration and fascination worldwide.

What are they?

  • The Great Wall of China
  • Petra: Located in Jordan
  • The Statue of Christ the Redeemer
  • Machu Picchu: Located in Peru
  • Chichen Itza: Located on the Yucatan Peninsula, Mexico
  • The Colosseum in Rome
  • The Taj Mahal: Located in the city of Agra, India

These seven wonders have left an indelible mark in history and continue to be popular tourist destinations today.
