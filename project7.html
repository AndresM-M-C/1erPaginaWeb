History of the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India. It was built between 1632 and 1653 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It is considered one of the most outstanding examples of Mughal architecture and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.

!The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in the city of Agra, in the state of Uttar Pradesh, in northern India. It was built between the years 1632 and 1653 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal is considered one of the most impressive architectural wonders in the world and has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its design combines elements of Islamic, Persian, Indian and Turkish architecture, and is an outstanding example of the Mughal style of architecture. The mausoleum is surrounded by a large garden that follows the Persian tradition of paradise gardens, with paths, fountains and reflecting pools. The main structure of the Taj Mahal is built of white marble, decorated with intricate details and raised calligraphy. It stands out for its central dome, which rises to a height of approximately 73 meters. The interior of the Taj Mahal houses the tombs of Shah Jahan and Mumtaz Mahal, as well as other tombs of members of the Mughal imperial family. The walls are adorned with inlaid precious stones and carved marble designs. The Taj Mahal receives millions of visitors each year and is an iconic symbol of India. It is a major tourist destination and a holy place for Muslims. Apart from its architectural beauty, Taj Mahal is also recognized as a monument of love as it was built as a tribute to Shah Jahan's beloved wife.
