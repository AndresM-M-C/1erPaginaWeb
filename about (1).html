Images of tourist areas

The most important areas of all Jilotepec plus a brief description of these


DANXHO DAM

The Danxho Dam is an artificial lagoon built to provide
water to a large part of the municipal seat of Jilotepec. However,
over the years it has been adapted to carry out water sports, such as sailboat races
, boat rides and diving.


THE PENAS

Las Penas is an ecotourism park located in the community
of Dexcani el Alto in Jilotepec.The region's unique
geographical formations make it attractive for extreme
activities , such as climbing, rappelling, mountain biking and hiking.


SEQUOIA PARK

In this park you will find children's games, and an esplanade for family recreation events. It is the ideal place to be in contact with nature and admire the birds that prostrate themselves in the vegetation. Visit this pleasant space with your loved ones!


CANALEJAS HILL

It is one of the oldest constructions that exist, it is a church formed on the top of a hill, built with “quarry” (carved stone), something also considered handmade. The Sanctuary of the Virgen de la Piedrita began to be built on December 15, 1897 and was finished on the same day but in 1981. Its construction took 84 long years. The church was covered with quarry from the region by the Canalejas master stonecutters.


PETROGLYPHS(THE EJIDO DE SAN LORENZO)

The petroglyphs of San Lorenzo represent the
cosmic observations of the original inhabitants
of Jilotepec.
The Otomi people were not used to building architecture
in stone, so these carved stones are a sign
that space was extremely important to them.
It is believed that the petroglyphs were an astronomical observatory.

PARISH OF SAINT PETER AND SAINT PAUL

Colonial-style parish,
built by the Franciscans in the 16th century, Accompanied
by a beautiful atrium, a cross of indigenous manufacture,
a baptismal font
carved into rock and a former Franciscan convent.
The parish of Saint Peter and Saint Paul was founded
by the Franciscans in the 16th century. In it you can appreciate a pulpit, a huge baptismal font in tequitqui style and some Marian monograms. Its façade
is simple, it was carved in stone and it has an
open chapel, dedicated to the ascension of Jesus Christ,
with an altarpiece that preserves seven oil paintings from the 18th century.
It also has a cube-shaped tower, with a clock.
Its convent is attached, you can visit the
cloister and, in its front part, you can see an indigenous cross
of rich iconography.


CENTRAL GARDEN OF JILOTEPEC

The central square of Jilotepec is the nucleus of
the urban life of the municipality. In this public space is the central garden; the fountain with a
eagle devouring a serpent perched on a nopal cactus, which represents the national coat of arms; the emblematic kiosk; a pillar with commemorative dates of the history of Mexico; as well as
the statue of Andrés Molina Enríquez, one of the
most important characters of Jilotepec.
